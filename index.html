Dinosaurs are extinct today because they lacked the opposable thumbs and brainpower to build a space program

Posted by Nabeel Mohammed on August 19,2025 ~4 mins read

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated the Earth for over 160 million years during the Mesozoic Era. They ranged in size from tiny bird-like creatures to massive, long-necked giants. Dinosaurs lived in a variety of environments, from lush forests to arid deserts, adapting to many ecological niches. Their fossilized remains have provided scientists with valuable insights into prehistoric life and evolution.

The extinction of dinosaurs occurred around 66 million years ago, likely due to a catastrophic asteroid impact and volcanic activity. This event paved the way for mammals to rise and eventually led to the evolution of humans. Despite their disappearance, dinosaurs continue to fascinate people through museum exhibits, movies, and scientific research. Modern birds are considered the closest living relatives of dinosaurs, linking the ancient past to the present.
